Your Character

Tabaxi · Monk · Warrior of the Open Hand · Criminal (Spy) — name still yours to choose!

You are a tabaxi — cat-folk: fur, tail, retractable claws, and a curiosity that has gotten you into every locked room you ever met. Somewhere along the way you trained your body into a weapon and your patience into armor. Officially, that discipline made you a monk. Unofficially, it made you the best second-story burglar money could hire — which is exactly how you used to pay the bills. Old habits die hard. Lucky for this crew, they don't need them to.

What is a Monk?

Monks fight with their body and win with their speed. No armor, no sword — your fists hit as hard as a longsword (1d8+4) and you get more attacks per turn than anyone at the table. Mechanically you're the party's skirmisher and scout: fastest mover, hardest to pin down, first through every window.

Your special resource is Focus Points — 5 of them, and here's the beautiful part: they come back on a short rest. You can afford to spend them every fight. They fuel your flashiest moves.

Your toolkit, in the order you'll use it

Speed. You move 40 ft per turn — and once per "stop", your Feline Agility doubles that. 80 ft in a turn, 160 with Dash. Nothing in this town outruns you.

Flurry of Blows (1 Focus Point, bonus action): two extra punches — so with your Attack action that's four attacks in one turn. And your Open Hand training lets each Flurry hit do a dirty trick: Topple them (prone — your friends hit them easier), Push them 15 ft (off a bridge, into the magma channel, out of the fight), or Addle them (no opportunity attacks — grab an ally and walk away free).

Stunning Strike (1 Focus Point, once per turn when you hit): CON save or the target is Stunned — it loses its turn and everyone hits it with advantage. This is the single scariest button a monk has. Even on a successful save its speed halves and the next attack has advantage.

Deflect Attacks (reaction): someone hits you with a weapon — you reduce the damage by 1d10+9. Average 14 damage just… slapped aside. Reduce it to zero and you can spend a Focus Point to throw the blow back (2d8+4 to someone nearby). Yes, you can catch a crossbow bolt and return it.

Uncanny Metabolism (1/day): when initiative is rolled you can refill ALL Focus Points and heal 1d8+5. Use it in the fight that matters.

Out of a fight — this is your real kingdom

You are the party's eyes and fingers: Stealth +7, Sleight of Hand +7, Acrobatics +7, Perception +6 (passive 16 — you notice everything), Thieves' Tools for locks and traps, a climbing speed, and Slow Fall (falling barely hurts you). Scout ahead, case the room, pick the lock, signal the crew. The heist runs through you.

Your weaknesses (know them, plan around them)

33 HP means you can't trade hits like Chumbo — your defense is not being there: Patient Defense (bonus action Disengage, or +Dodge for 1 FP) keeps you slippery. You have no ranged attack worth mentioning, so pick your entrances. And WIS-save monsters aside, your INT and CHA are ordinary — leave the talking to the nobles, you're the one on the roof.

Totally lost? One of these is always fine:

  1. ACTION Attack twice (+7, 1d8+4 each) → BONUS Flurry (1 FP): two more punches, and Topple someone prone.
  2. Something hits you? REACTION Deflect Attacks — subtract 1d10+9 from the damage. Always. Every round.
  3. Bad spot? BONUS Patient Defense (free Disengage) and walk away — or Step of the Wind and be 80 ft away.
