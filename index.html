How D&D Works

The two-minute version — everything else you can ask at the table

The golden rule: you don't need to know rules. Say what your character tries — "I want to sneak past the guard", "I check the desk for hidden drawers", "I lie to his face" — and the DM tells you what to roll. The rules find you.

The three rolls (it's always a d20)

Ability check
Can you do a thing? Roll d20 + skill (your sheet's numbers) vs a difficulty the DM sets. Climbing, lying, spotting, remembering.
Attack roll
Do you hit? Roll d20 + attack bonus vs the target's Armor Class (AC). Hit → roll damage.
Saving throw
Can you resist? Something bad targets you, you roll d20 + save vs the effect's DC. Dodge the fireball, resist the charm.
Advantage / Disadvantage: roll TWO d20s, take the better / worse one. That's the whole mechanic. A natural 20 on an attack is a crit: double the damage dice.

Two modes of play

Free play most of the session
Exploring, talking, scheming — no turns, no order. Just talk; jump in whenever. Time flows however the story needs.
Combat when swords come out
Time freezes into rounds (~6 seconds each). Everyone rolls initiative (d20 + your Init) once; you act in that order every round until the fight ends. On your turn: your budget below. Off your turn: you can still use a REACTION.

Your turn — a budget, not a script

MOVEyour speed in feet — split it freely: move, attack, keep moving
ACTIONyour ONE big thing: Attack, cast a spell, Dash (double move), Dodge (hard to hit), Help, Hide, Utilize an object
BONUSONE small extra — but only if an ability specifically grants one (they're marked green on your sheet). No green ability, no bonus action.
FREEinteract with one object on the way: draw a weapon, open a door, drop the torch
REACTIONONE per round, usually on OTHER people's turns — an enemy walks away from you (free swipe!), or an ability marked violet triggers
Example turn: move 15 ft to the goon (MOVE), attack him (ACTION), Healing Word the fallen friend (BONUS — a green ability), kick a chair into the doorway (FREE). Later, on the goon's turn, he runs past Theron — Theron uses his REACTION to hit him.

Spellcasting in 30 seconds

Spell slots
Casting a leveled spell spends a slot of that level or higher (higher often = stronger). Slots refill on a long rest. Cantrips are free, always.
Concentration C
Some spells stay active only while you concentrate. Only one at a time — casting a second C-spell drops the first. Taking damage → CON save (DC 10 or half the damage) or it drops.
Rituals [R]
Cast without a slot if you take 10 extra minutes. Great outside combat.

Getting hurt

0 HP
You fall Unconscious — not dead. On your turns you roll death saves (plain d20): 10+ is a success. Three successes = stable; three fails = dead. A nat 20 = wake up with 1 HP; a nat 1 = two fails.
Healing
ANY healing (even 1 HP) wakes you up. This is why Gimli and Laurald scream "stay in range!"
Resting
Short rest (1 h): spend Hit Dice to heal; some abilities recharge. Long rest (8 h): everything back — HP, slots, the works.
Conditions you'll hear (what they actually mean)
ProneOn the ground. Melee attacks vs you have advantage, ranged disadvantage. Half your movement to stand up.
GrappledHeld — speed 0. Escape: action, Athletics or Acrobatics check.
RestrainedTied/webbed: can't move, attacks vs you advantage, yours disadvantage, DEX saves disadvantage.
FrightenedDisadvantage on everything while you see the source; can't move closer to it.
CharmedCan't attack the charmer; they have social advantage on you.
BlindedAttacks vs you advantage, yours disadvantage, auto-fail sight checks.
InvisibleReverse of blinded: you attack with advantage, enemies at disadvantage.
IncapacitatedNo actions, no reactions. Paralyzed/Stunned/Unconscious include this and get worse from there (paralyzed: melee hits auto-crit!).
Difficult terrainRubble, steam pipes, lava crust: every foot costs two.

New-player survival tips

  1. Plan your turn while others take theirs — combat stays snappy.
  2. You can always just Attack or Dodge. Nobody optimizes at level 5.
  3. Describe the attempt, not the rule. "I slide the flask off the desk into my sleeve" beats "can I roll Sleight of Hand?"
  4. Rule of Cool is real: creative and dramatic beats optimal at this table.
  5. Tap any underlined name↗ on your character sheet for the full official text on 5e.tools.
