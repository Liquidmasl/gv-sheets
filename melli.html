Your Character

Tiefling · Sorcerer · Shadow Magic · Noble — name still yours to choose!

You were born into a noble house — fine clothes, a signet ring, a name that opens doors. You were also born a tiefling, with horns, a tail, and an infernal bloodline the family prefers not to discuss at dinner. And somewhere in that bloodline, something darker than hellfire stirred: the Shadowfell, the plane of gloom, left its fingerprint on your soul. Magic doesn't come to you from books or gods. It simply happens — and lately, the shadows have started answering back.

What is a Sorcerer?

Wizards study magic. Clerics pray for it. You just have it. It's in your blood, and your Charisma — your force of personality — is what shapes it. Mechanically you're the party's artillery and trickster: you throw the biggest damage in the group and warp the battlefield, but you're fragile, so you do it from the back row.

Your special resource is Sorcery Points (SP) — 5 per day. Think of them as raw, liquid magic: you can burn them to bend your spells (Metamagic), convert them into extra spell slots, or fuel your shadow powers. Managing SP is your little economy game.

Your shadow side

Your subclass (Shadow Magic) gives you three quiet superpowers:

1. Eyes of the Dark. You see 120 ft in darkness — twice as far as other darkvision. The dark is your home turf.

2. The Darkness trick. You know the Darkness spell, and when you cast it with 2 sorcery points (instead of a slot), you can see through your own darkness. Nobody else can — not friends, not enemies. Drop it on a fight and you're the only person in the room with eyes. (Mako's Devil's Sight also pierces it — you two are natural partners in crime.)

3. Strength of the Grave. Once per day, when damage would drop you to 0 HP, you can make a Charisma save to stay at 1 HP instead. The Shadowfell doesn't want you back yet.

How a fight usually goes

Round one: if things look serious, use your Bonus Action for Innate Sorcery (your spells get harder to resist and your spell attacks get advantage for a minute). Then throw damage: Fireball for groups, Sorcerous Burst or Force Blast (your at-will cantrips, both hit like crossbows) for single targets, Magic Missile when you absolutely cannot afford to miss. Keep Shield in your pocket — it's a reaction that adds +5 AC when something would hit you.

And the party trick: your Careful Spell metamagic (1 SP) lets you drop a Fireball on a melee everyone's tangled in and choose up to 4 friends to automatically pass the save. Chumbo will love you.

Out of a fight

You're a noble with Persuasion +7 — alongside Theron you're the party's voice. Your cantrips are a utility belt: Prestidigitation (small magical anything), Mage Hand (invisible floating hand), Message (whisper to anyone within 120 ft, they can whisper back). You also carry a crowbar. Nobles contain multitudes.

Your weaknesses (know them, plan around them)

You are squishy. 32 HP and AC 11 naked — so cast Mage Armor every morning (one 1st-level slot, lasts 8 hours, AC becomes 14). Stay behind Chumbo and Theron, use range, and remember Misty Step (bonus action teleport) is your escape hatch. If someone grabs you, that's what Shield, Misty Step, and screaming for the paladin are for.

Still open — talk to the DM

Your name, your look, and your story: which noble house? Do they know about the shadow thing? Why are you doing heists instead of attending balls? Bring ideas to the table — or discover the answers mid-game. Both work.
